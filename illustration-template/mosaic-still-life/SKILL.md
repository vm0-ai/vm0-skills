# /mosaic-still-life — locked mosaic-tile + painterly hybrid style

This skill produces editorial still-life illustrations in a hybrid style where **mosaic-tile/tessellated surfaces** carry the background and ground, while **painterly objects with crisp ink outlines** sit on top. The technique evokes the look of a hand-glazed ceramic tile mural overlaid with a gouache painting — cozy, nostalgic, slightly naive, deeply atmospheric.

Each invocation swaps a few user-adjustable dials (palette, scene, animal companion, mosaic grain, point of view). Every other style axis stays locked.

## Prompt interpretation

The user gives a short brief — usually palette + scene + animal (e.g. "moonlit garden, black cat, indigo palette"). It is your job to **brainstorm and expand it into a rich, specific still-life moment**. Do not stop to confirm. Imagine the scene vividly and run.

For each invocation:

1. **Take the user's short brief as the seed.** Treat it as the starting hook, not the whole composition.
2. **Pick the dial values:**
   - Palette → from validated palettes below or a sibling
   - Scene → outdoor or indoor; outdoor uses grass/sky/sand as the mosaic field, indoor uses pegboard/color-swatch wall/tile backsplash/brick/checkered floor
   - Animal → cat / dog / bunny / bird, fitted to the scene's mood
   - Mosaic grain → fine (painterly), medium (ceramic-tile), or chunky (pixel-art)
   - POV → 3/4 angle (cozy/intimate) or top-down (active/narrative)
3. **Compose the still life.** 5-9 objects on a table or surface, arranged loosely. Include food/drink, a vessel of greenery or flowers, at least one container with a label or pattern, and small character props (a folded napkin, a sliced fruit, a tiny spoon, a candle).
4. **Place the animal companion** so it anchors the emotional center — sitting on a chair, curled on a cushion, peeking from under the table, perched on the counter.
5. **Identify the patterned textile** that peeks through — chair cushion, tablecloth corner, draped napkin, throw blanket.
6. **Keep the style brief style-locked.** The only creative variation should be the dial values and the scene-specific props.

Bias toward concrete, lived-in details: a half-bitten croissant, a tiny pencil beside the open book, a wisp of steam off a teacup, a single lemon rolled off the bowl. The scene should feel like the artist *was there*.

### 2. SCENE
Anchor the still life somewhere specific. Validated scenes — split by mosaic surface type:

**Outdoor (the mosaic lives in grass/sky/sand/water):**
- Garden table on a lawn (mosaic grass)

- Beach picnic blanket (mosaic sand + mosaic horizon sea)
- Moonlit garden with starfield (mosaic night sky with tiled stars + crescent moon)

**Indoor (the mosaic lives in wallpaper, pegboard, tile backsplash, brick, checkered floor, bookshelves):**
- Reading nook by a window (mosaic windowpanes showing tessellated sky/trees)
- Cottage by the fireplace (mosaic brick wall + tiled floor)
- Woodworking workshop (mosaic pegboard + mosaic brick floor)
- Painter's studio (mosaic color-swatch wall — the mosaic becomes *diegetic*, literally a painter's color study)

- Pantry, mudroom, sunroom

**The principle:** every space has at least one surface that wants to be tessellated. The mosaic doesn't need nature — it needs **modular repetition**, which is everywhere in human-made spaces too.

### 3. ANIMAL COMPANION
The heart of the scene. Validated: tabby cat, ginger cat, calico cat, charcoal-striped cat, black cat, corgi, border collie, basset hound, lop-eared bunny, seagull. Pick one whose temperament fits the mood (sleepy tabby for reading nook, alert collie for workshop, regal black cat for moonlit garden).

The animal is painted in the **same crisp ink-outline + soft-fur painted style** as the rest of the still life. Never rendered as mosaic. Eyes are expressive — closed-smile for sleepy scenes, bright open for alert scenes.

### 4. MOSAIC GRAIN
- **Fine** — small tiles, painterly/postcard feel. Best for atmospheric scenes (dusk, moonlight, indoors).
- **Medium** — visible square tiles, real-ceramic feel. Best for warm interiors, brick, terracotta floors.
- **Chunky** — large blocky tiles, strong pixel-art read. Best for sandy beaches or bold playful scenes.

### 5. POV
- **Slight 3/4 angle** — cozy/intimate. The table is partially seen from the side, you feel like a guest pulling up a chair. Default.
- **Top-down** — active/narrative. Picnic blankets, prep counters, board games. Opens up the still life as a flat composition.

## Locked style elements (NEVER vary)

### Mosaic surfaces (the foundation)
- At least one large background or ground surface is rendered as **small tessellated square tiles** in varied tones of the chosen palette
- The mosaic mimics the look of irregular hand-glazed ceramic — slight tonal variation tile-to-tile, no perfectly uniform grid, occasional darker or lighter accent tiles
- For outdoor scenes, the mosaic IS the grass/sky/sand. For indoor scenes, the mosaic IS the wallpaper, pegboard, backsplash, brick, or color-swatch grid
- Some scenes can layer **two mosaic surfaces** for texture density (e.g. mosaic pegboard above + mosaic brick floor below; mosaic backsplash above + mosaic checkered floor below)

### Painterly objects ON TOP
- Still-life objects (food, vessels, candles, books, tools, the animal) are **NOT mosaic** — they're rendered painterly with crisp ink outlines and soft gouache-style color fills
- Objects sit visually ON TOP of the mosaic surface, never blending into it
- Outlines are slightly imperfect, hand-painted, with subtle pressure variation — not vector-clean

### Animal companion
- One friendly animal at the emotional center of the scene
- Same painterly style as the objects
- Soft fur detail, expressive eyes, calm body language

### Patterned textile peeking through
- At least one textile shows a small pattern — striped chair cushion, gingham picnic blanket, plaid tablecloth corner, paisley throw, woven rug under the table, embroidered napkin edge
- Adds another layer of pattern density beneath the painterly objects

### Still-life centerpiece
- A table, counter, blanket, workbench, or other flat surface holds 5-9 small objects arranged loosely
- Always include: a vessel of greenery/flowers, a drink, a food item, a labeled container, small character props (spoon, candle, folded napkin, pencil, ruler)
- Items chosen to fit the scene (breakfast scene → croissant + jam jar; workshop scene → hand plane + nail jar + blueprint)

### Mood
- Cozy, nostalgic, bucolic, hushed
- Never busy or frenetic; the scene feels like a calm captured moment
- Reads like a magazine spread or storybook page

### Output format
- Portrait orientation default, 1024x1536
- No text labels (other than tiny incidental handwritten labels on a jam jar, etc.)
- Gouache + digital mosaic hybrid feel

## Composition rules
- Animal companion is the emotional center
- Still-life centerpiece is the visual center
- Mosaic surface fills the negative space (background, ground, or both)
- Patterned textile draped or visible somewhere
- Generous breathing room — never crowd the still life
- Slight off-center composition allowed; perfectly symmetric arrangements feel stiff

## Generation approach

This style is best produced by an **image-to-image** model with a strong reference image and a high input-fidelity setting. The reference image carries the mosaic-tessellation cue that text alone struggles to specify.

- **Reference image**: pass one of the validated `ref-*.jpg` files in this directory as the source. Pick the one whose palette/mood is closest to the user's brief — the generator will inherit its mosaic-grain calibration.
- **Input fidelity**: high — the mosaic technique is fragile and easily lost without strong style anchoring.
- **Quality**: high — fine mosaic grain requires resolution to read correctly.
- **Size**: portrait 1024x1536 by default.

Known-good model: `gpt-image-2` via fal.ai. Other strong painterly models with i2i support may also work; calibrate input fidelity to preserve the mosaic ground.

If a model rejects high input fidelity or strips the mosaic on first pass, increase emphasis on "tessellated/mosaic ground rendered as small square tiles, pointillist" in the prompt, and explicitly call out the surface that should carry the mosaic.

## Style correction guide

If the output misses on a specific axis, use a correction focused on that one issue:

| Issue | Correction |
|---|---|
| Mosaic surface missing / blended | "Replace the {{SURFACE}} with explicit small tessellated square tiles in varied tones — pointillist mosaic technique, slightly irregular hand-glazed ceramic feel, like the grass in the reference." |
| Objects rendered as mosaic too | "The still-life objects should be painterly with crisp ink outlines, NOT mosaic. Only the background/ground surface is tessellated. Objects sit visually on top." |
| Animal rendered as mosaic | "The animal companion is painterly with soft fur and crisp ink outlines — same style as the still-life objects, NEVER mosaic." |
| Grain too tight / too loose | "Increase/decrease the tile size to {{fine / medium / chunky}} grain — smaller tiles for painterly feel, larger tiles for pixel-art feel." |
| Palette drifted | "Re-render with the strict palette: {{PALETTE LIST}}. Remove other hues. Keep at least one creamy neutral so objects read on top." |
| Mood too clinical / designed | "Looser, more hand-painted, slightly naive — like a children's book magazine spread. Imperfect mosaic edges, hand-painted outlines, breathing whitespace within the composition." |
| Animal feels disconnected | "Reposition the animal at the emotional center of the scene — on the chair, curled on the table, peeking from below. It should anchor the viewer's eye." |
| Missing patterned textile | "Add a small patterned textile peeking through somewhere — striped cushion on the chair, gingham blanket corner, plaid napkin, woven rug under the table." |
| Still life feels sparse / crowded | "Adjust to 5-9 objects loosely arranged on the surface, breathing room between items, no crowding. Include a vessel of greenery, a drink, a food item, a labeled container, small character props." |
